About Me

I'm a Computer Science graduate from Boston University, with a minor in Business Administration. My path has been deliberate — I gravitate toward work that's both technical and meaningful, especially at the intersection of security and software.

On the security side, I've built and operated a full Wazuh SIEM homelab — executing exploitation chains, writing custom detection rules, and mapping findings to the MITRE ATT&CK framework. On the dev side, I shipped a full Android social app in Kotlin with Firebase and a custom camera pipeline.

I care about who bears the consequences of automated decisions. That's part of why security appeals to me — it's one of the few fields where thinking carefully about adversarial systems is the whole job.
